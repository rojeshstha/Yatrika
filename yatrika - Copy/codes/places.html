Trek Overview: How It Begins and Ends

The Manaslu Circuit Trek starts with a scenic drive from Kathmandu to Machha Khola. The trail follows the Budhi Gandaki River through lush forests, rice terraces, and traditional Gurung and Tibetan villages. As the trek ascends, the landscape becomes alpine, and the villages take on strong Tibetan cultural influences. The trail passes through Samagaon and Samdo, where acclimatization days allow you to explore beautiful monasteries and viewpoints. The trek culminates in crossing the breathtaking Larkya La Pass (5,160 m), one of the highest trekking passes in Nepal, offering expansive views of the Manaslu and Annapurna ranges. The descent leads to Dharapani, from where a private vehicle brings you back to Kathmandu.

Best Time for the Manaslu Circuit Trek

  • Spring (March–May): Pleasant temperatures and rhododendron blooms.
  • Autumn (Sept–Nov): Stable weather, crisp air, and best mountain views.
  • Winter (Dec–Feb): Pass may be blocked due to snow.
  • Monsoon (June–Aug): Trails can be slippery; not ideal for this remote route.

Climate and Weather

  • Spring: 10°C to 20°C daytime, 0°C to -5°C at higher elevations at night.
  • Autumn: 10°C to 18°C daytime, -2°C to -6°C at night in higher regions.
  • Winter: -5°C to 10°C daytime, -10°C or below at night at high altitudes.
  • Monsoon: Heavy rainfall; not recommended for this trek.

  • Day 8: Trek to Samdo (3,860 m) – 4 hrs

  • Day 10: Trek to Dharamsala (4,460 m) – 4 hrs

  • Day 11: Cross Larkya La Pass (5,160 m) and trek to Bimthang (3,720 m) – 8 hrs

  • Day 12: Trek to Tilije (2,300 m) – 5 hrs

  • Day 13: Trek to Dharapani and drive to Besisahar – 6 hrs

  • Day 14: Drive from Besisahar to Kathmandu – 6 hrs
